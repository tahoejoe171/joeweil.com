Prediction Market Bot


There is an old joke that goes as follows: Two economists are walking down the street and pass by a $100 bill on the ground without picking it up. The first economist asks the other: "Was that a hundred dollar bill on the ground?" to which the other replies, "Nope, if it was, someone would have picked it up already".

I often think that this notion that good ideas have already been capitalized on should prevent me from taking on any risk for fear of wasting my time, and nowhere is that more true than with trading in asset markets. I have always wanted to develop a fully-fledged autonomous system to print money for me, but as anyone might reasonably think, successfully competing with bots worldwide is a total pipe dream. However with algorithmic trading, the total lack of willingness of anyone to share any methodologies or "alpha" for fear of losing one's profitability has made me wonder if I can develop my own. Surely, a unique mix of signals derived from a plethora of my own data and mathematical predictions could offer me a subset of market conditions which, if traded properly, could yield some profit. Ultimately, I do not expect to get rich off a probabilistic machine, however at the very least, I could develop my own system, improve my programming and development skills, and create a worthwhile project to showcase here.

This project is a fully autonomous prediction market trading bot built in Python, designed to pipeline live market data, conduct quantitative analysis, and execute trades on prediction market event derivatives for 15-minute cryptocurrency markets. The system runs continuously on local hardware, ingesting and preprocessing market data in real time, applying statistical forecasting models to assess outcome probabilities, and executing trades based on systematic decision logic. The core objective was to build an end-to-end data pipeline that could independently identify mispriced market opportunities and act on them without manual intervention. Key technical components include a live data ingestion pipeline, quantitative probability modeling and backtesting engine, and automated trade execution, all implemented in Python. This project is mostly complete, and documentation is in progress.

  • Develop a data pipeline for hands-off prediction market data collection
  • Achieve consistent positive expected value across a diversified set of market positions
  • Ensure system reliability and uptime on permanent local hardware
  • Develop and refine custom probabilistic forecasting models to identify market inefficiencies
  • Profit?

The foundation of this system is a series of coroutines which poll live websocket data streams from Coinbase (BTC price) and Kalshi (yes/no bid and ask prices). A dedicated REST poller runs in parallel as a source of truth, which refreshes market prices to prevent collecting stale data. The streams feed into a 0.5-second sampler which records market state and price snapshots, operates the forecasting logic, and executes trades. A separate backtesting engine is used periodically to simulate live trading across the whole dataset and optimize constraints.

  • single_stream_market: Maintains a live kalshi websocket stream for one market ticker
  • stream_market: time-driven lifecycle manager that pre-fetches, warms up, and promotes new markets at ever 15 minute boundary
  • sample_price: Samples all market state every 0.5s, operates the prediction model, and appends rows to current_data
  • watchdog: logs a heartbeat every 30s to prevent stale market values
  • stream_prices: maintains a live BTC/USD price feed from Coinbase websocket
  • _seed_from_rest: fetches the current best bid/ask from Kalshi REST API immediately after websocket subscription to eliminate cold-start gap
  • periodic_save: saves data ever 2 hours as a background task
  • write_current_data: blocking worker that writes current_data snapshot to its temp CSV file
  • schedule_save_current_data: non-blocking dispatcher that offloads the write_current_data to a thread
  • place_order: uses the Kalshi API to place predictions on my account
  • main: initializes globals, resolves the current market, registers signal handlers, and launches all asynchronous coroutines
  • simulator: systematically runs the dataset through a one-to-one live market simulation, optimizing constraints and variables for profit
  • Check the dataset for missing target price values and poll the Kalshi API to fill missing values with those from the resolved markets
  • Identically recreate the internal market conditions for every complete market in the dataset
  • Run predictive modeling using customizable parameters and market condition constraints
  • Compile the many qualitative/quantitative output fields into a results dataset for direct comparison of parameter sets
  • Simulate thousands of sets of parameters using threading

  • Maintaining simultaneous persistent connections to Kalshi and Coinbase's price feeds, handling disconnectoins, timeouts, and reconnection logic without data corruption or I/O blocking
  • Large-scale dataset assessment and log-combing to troubleshoot data pipeline bugs over the course of months and tens of millions of data rows
  • Maintaining atomicity across four derived data fields that are independently updated in the underlying feed in order to prevent spread inversion/corruption
  • Corrdinating a precise 15-minute market boundary transition with absolutely zero data loss or overlap
  • Ensuring Idempotency in fill-or-kill order placement under retry
  • Identifying and accurately quanitfying mispriced market conditions based on quantifications of my own design
  • Reproducing the identical internal state of the live trading system at any point from a CSV, including premarket seeding, time derivation, coverage calculations, and benchmark index lookups
  • Calibrating predictive modeling against millions of Monte Carlo simulations and ensuring genuine predictive edge/profitability with statistical modeling
